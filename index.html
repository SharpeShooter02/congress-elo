Congressional Trading ELO

Every disclosed trade is scored as a head-to-head match against the S&P 500: a buy that beats the market wins, and a well-timed sell — one made before the stock lags the market — also wins. Each trade's edge is time-decayed (a move within weeks counts far more than one that takes years), so quick, repeatable correctness rises to the top. Ratings start at 1500 against a fixed 1500-rated market. The Buys and Sells columns split each member's record, so you can see whether a rating comes from buying skill or from selling ahead of declines. Click any row to reveal the specific trades carrying that rating. A 🚩 marks members who've made sharp calls — trades that beat the market 15%+ within a month, a flag for scrutiny (not proof).

Fixed opponent — S&P 500 = 1500 ELO. Above 1500 beats the market more often than not.

Methodology & hosting

The match. Each disclosed trade's return is compared to the S&P 500's over the same period — the excess return — blended across the ~30-day, ~1-year, and full holding windows and time-decayed so fast moves count most and slow buy-and-holds count least. For a Purchase, positive excess = win; for a Sale, the stock lagging the market afterward = win. ±0.5% is a tie.

Rating update. ELO with the market fixed at 1500: E = 1/(1+10^((1500−R)/700)), margin multiplier m = min(1+ln(1+|excess%|), 4.5), and R += 32·m·(S−E) with S = 1/0.5/0. Trades replay in chronological order. The Buys/Sells columns split each record by trade direction, so a mediocre buyer who sells with suspicious timing is visible at a glance.

Sharp calls 🚩. A trade is flagged a sharp call when it beat the S&P by 15%+ within about a month of the disclosure — fast, decisive correctness of the kind non-public information tends to produce (e.g. a Department of Energy official selling an energy stock right before it slid). The 🚩 next to a name counts how many they've made. Treat it as a statistical flag worth a closer look, not proof of wrongdoing — fast wins also come from skill, luck, or public news.

Data flow. build_leaderboard.py (run in GitHub Actions) pulls the open Congress Trading Monitor dataset — House, Senate, and executive-branch filings — computes the ELO, and writes data.json + data.js. This page fetches data.json when hosted (Reload pulls the latest), falling back to data.js for local opens or synthetic sample data.
