Congressional Trading ELO

Each disclosed trade is scored as a head-to-head match against the S&P 500: a buy that outperforms the market over the holding window wins; a sell that dodges underperformance wins. Ratings start at 1500 and move by a margin-weighted ELO update. The market is a fixed 1500-rated opponent, so a member climbs only by consistently beating it.

Fixed opponent — S&P 500 = 1500 ELO. Above 1500 beats the market more often than not.

Methodology & hosting

The match. For every disclosed trade we take the traded stock's return over a fixed holding window (default 30 trading days) and subtract the S&P 500's return over the exact same window — the excess return. For a Purchase, positive excess = win; for a Sale, negative excess (sold before relative weakness) = win. ±0.5% is a tie.

Rating update. ELO with the market fixed at 1500: E = 1/(1+10^((1500−R)/400)), margin multiplier m = min(1+ln(1+|excess%|), 3), and R += 32·m·(S−E) with S = 1/0.5/0. Trades replay in chronological order.

Data flow. build_leaderboard.py (run locally or in GitHub Actions) pulls Stock Watcher trades + yfinance EOD prices, computes the ELO, and writes data.json + data.js. This page fetches data.json when hosted (Reload button pulls the latest), and falls back to data.js for local file:// opens, or to synthetic sample data if neither is present.
